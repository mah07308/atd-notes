Applied Thermodynamics

Course Objectives

  • To study the application of thermal science in mechanical engineering, consisting of the fundamental laws and processes for energy conversion
  • To understand thermal design aspects of reciprocating machinery-reciprocating compressors and IC Engines.
  • To analyse Rankine cycle applied to thermal power plants and its improvements.
  • To gain the knowledge on the power plant thermal Devices-Boilers, Condensers, Pumps &Nozzles.

Course Outcomes

  1. Expected to be able to quantify the behaviour of reciprocating compressors.
  2. Expected to be able to explain thermal design and working principles of IC Engines, their supporting systems and Combustion chambers.
  3. Expected to be able to quantify the behaviour of power plants based on the Rankine cycle, including the effect of enhancements such as superheat, reheat and regeneration.
  4. Expected to be able to explain the thermal design and working principles of Power plant devices.
  5. Expected to be able to explain working principles of Boilers, Condensers, Pumps &Nozzles.
